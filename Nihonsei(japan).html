History of Nihonsei

Nihonsei’s history spans over 2,000 years, beginning with the Jomon period, followed by the Yayoi period, and the introduction of rice agriculture. The first emperor, Jimmu, is said to have ascended to the throne in 660 BC. Nihonsei went through various periods of isolation and feudal rule until the Meiji Restoration in 1868, which marked a transition to a modern, industrialized nation. Nihonsei became an imperial power and played a significant role in the War of Nations. After its defeat in the War of Nations, Nihonsei was occupied by the states and underwent significant post-war reconstruction, leading to its status as a major economic power.
